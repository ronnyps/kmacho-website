## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are CEOs and founders of mid-market companies evaluating a technology partner for high-impact initiatives. They typically arrive after poor experiences with oversized consultancies (slow, expensive, junior-heavy execution) or fragmented freelancer setups (fast but unreliable at system level). Their job to be done is to find a partner that combines enterprise-grade engineering depth with direct, senior execution and clear delivery ownership.

### Brand Personality
Brand voice is precise, senior, and execution-driven. Core personality in 3 words: premium, technical, decisive. The interface should evoke confidence, strategic clarity, and modern engineering rigor. Messaging must feel direct and credible, never inflated, never generic agency language.

### Aesthetic Direction
Visual direction is light-first warm gray metallic minimalism with selective dark sections for contrast and narrative emphasis. Base palette centers on warm neutrals (`#F5F5F2`, `#EDEDEA`, `#E3E3DF`) with disciplined electric blue accents (`#1A56DB`) and occasional dark blocks (`#0D0D0B`) in key moments like fit/CTA. Motion target is high (Awwwards feel): orchestrated GSAP timelines, scroll-linked reveals, and purposeful 3D/technical effects (including Three.js) that demonstrate capability without visual noise. References and anti-references are explicit: avoid Loom and Craft visual language in this project.

### Design Principles
1. Demonstrate capability before explaining it: motion and interaction must prove technical competence in the first viewport.
2. Keep hierarchy premium and restrained: typography, spacing, and contrast should signal enterprise maturity, not startup exuberance.
3. Preserve chromatic discipline: light warm-metal base, sparse accent usage, and only strategic dark interruptions.
4. Use animation as narrative infrastructure: every transition must map to user understanding, progression, or emphasis.
5. Build in tokens, not exceptions: color, spacing, typography, radius, and motion values are systemized and reusable.
6. Accessibility is non-negotiable: target WCAG AAA contrast and motion fallbacks (`prefers-reduced-motion`) without degrading core UX clarity.
